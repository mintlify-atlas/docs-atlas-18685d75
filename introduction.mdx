The Wiki Education Dashboard is a web application that supports Wikipedia education assignments, edit-a-thons, and other wiki editing programs. It helps instructors create and manage courses, track student contributions across Wikipedia and other Wikimedia wikis, and provide training resources — all in one place.

The Dashboard powers two production sites:
- **[dashboard.wikiedu.org](https://dashboard.wikiedu.org)** — for Wiki Education programs focused on higher education in the United States and Canada
- **[outreachdashboard.wmflabs.org](https://outreachdashboard.wmflabs.org)** — for the global Wikimedia community to organize edit-a-thons, education programs, and other events

## What the Dashboard does

<Steps>
  <Step title="Instructors create a course">
    An instructor or program organizer creates a course page, sets dates, and receives a shareable enroll link with a passcode.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Students join and get assigned articles">
    Students join the course using the enroll link. The assignment wizard helps instructors build a structured timeline of weekly tasks and article assignments.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The Dashboard tracks contributions">
    As students edit Wikipedia, the Dashboard automatically imports their revision data — article edits, bytes added, references added, and article quality scores.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Instructors review progress">
    The course dashboard surfaces key metrics: articles edited, words added, article views, and training completion. Alerts notify instructors of issues like suspected plagiarism or unsubmitted drafts.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Key features

- **Multi-wiki support** — Track edits across Wikipedia (all languages), Wikidata, Wikimedia Commons, and other Wikimedia projects
- **Assignment wizard** — Build structured course timelines with weekly blocks and article assignments
- **Training modules** — Deliver self-paced training content to students directly in the Dashboard
- **Real-time analytics** — Article quality scores via Lift Wing API, pageview data, and revision-level statistics
- **Plagiarism detection** — Automatic alerts for suspected copyright violations via CopyPatrol
- **Campaigns** — Group related courses together for aggregate reporting and program-wide analytics
- **Internationalization** — Interface available in many languages via translatewiki.net

<Note>
  The Dashboard uses Wikipedia accounts for authentication. Users log in with their Wikipedia credentials via OAuth — no separate account creation is needed.
</Note>
